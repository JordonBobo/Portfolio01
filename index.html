This page is devoted to my transition to becoming a coder or web developer.

Although I have had a lifelong interest in computers and coding, and even built my first computer at age 10, my experiences in high school and college encouraged me to pursue my other passions such as history, agriculture, and linguistics. After getting my bachelors degree from Utah State University, I initially attempted to pursue a career in archiving and library science, and spent nearly 4 years working at the Murray Library. While I was there, I helped facilitate the procurement and deployment of a new Integrated Library System. That project rekindled my appreciation for software development and I decided to pursue coding and analytics which brought me to my current employer.

I work as a Systems Support Specialist for the Utah Higher Education Assistance Authority, which is part of the Utah System of Higher Education. Every day I get to work with some pretty great people to help make sure that education is available to anyone and everyone. I work mostly behind the scenes monitoring and troubleshooting software and computer scripts that were developed in-house, granting and revoking access to various databases, programs, and networks, as well as assisting employees and clients with our company’s software.

In order to improve my skills and continue to pivot my career toward software and coding, I am currently engaged in the University of Utah’s Coding BootCamp in order to advance my skills in full stack development. My original goal was to use the skills learned in the bootcamp to improve my position with my current employer, but opportunities to join the Application Development team are very limited. Rather than being content to wait a year or more before I can actually start working as a developer, I am actively seeking, with the knowledge and full support of my boss and co-workers, any opportunities to work in the field of software development.
